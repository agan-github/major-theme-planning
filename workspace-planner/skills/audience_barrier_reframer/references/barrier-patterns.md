# Barrier Patterns

## Common barrier types

- abstract-concept overload
- institution-centered framing
- internal shorthand
- conclusion-first framing
- low relevance
- evidence-thin framing
- trust-distance
- comparison mismatch

## Diagnostic rule

Identify the one barrier that would most likely stop the audience in the first few lines.

## Strong reframing signals

- recognizable issue entry
- lower background burden
- practical relevance
- visible evidence path
- preserved distinctiveness

## Weak reframing signals

- one slogan replaced by another
- wording simplified but structure unchanged
- generic relatability with no real issue logic
