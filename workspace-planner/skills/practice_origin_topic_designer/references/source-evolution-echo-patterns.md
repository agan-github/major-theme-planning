# Source Evolution Echo Patterns

## What counts as source

A source point is a formative practical setting where the idea, method, or governing logic was shaped, tested, or made visible.

A good source point has:
- formative timing
- a real problem
- identifiable decision logic
- continuity potential
- reporting legibility

## What counts as evolution

Evolution is how the original logic expands, deepens, scales, or becomes more systematic.

## What counts as echo

Echo is present-day evidence that makes the original logic visible now.

## Strong-case signals

- clear place-based anchor
- visible before-and-after contrast
- long time depth
- identifiable decisions
- present-day relevance
- scene and interview potential

## Weak-case signals

- only conceptual importance
- no true anchor
- no current evidence
- no continuity
- no scene support
